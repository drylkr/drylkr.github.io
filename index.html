Hello, I'm Daryl Ong

I’m a full-stack developer drawn to how systems work, from web apps and analytics to the security layers underneath them.

My tech journey started at thirteen on Tumblr, where I spent an unreasonable amount of time customizing themes, adding music players, and making entire pages sparkle for no practical reason.

I accidentally got into tech in high school after ditching a cooking class because my friends ended up elsewhere. Someone said there were open slots in a computer class and I went, “sure.” That random decision somehow turned into studying hardware/software servicing, joining Microsoft DigiGirlz, and eventually pursuing Computer Engineering.

I also went through a harmless “script kiddie” phase writing prank scripts for friends. Nothing destructive, mostly just enough chaos to make someone question why their desktop suddenly started talking.

Outside of tech, I collect hobbies like side quests: playlists, painting, occasional piano/guitar sessions, and convincing people to download Brawl Stars. I’ve recruited three friends so far, which might be my greatest achievement in tech.

Looking back, it’s funny that this all started with a Tumblr phase and one impulsive class switch. I’m still figuring things out, but I’ve realized I really enjoy building things that sit somewhere between systems, creativity, and problem-solving.
